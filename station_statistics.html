Statistics#

This page presents statistics of solar irradiance monitoring stations using data from SolarStations.Org.

Let’s take a closer look at the current active stations and their measurement records.

The following conclusions can be drawn from the figures above:

  • There are currently 431 active stations out of the 787 known stations

  • Of the active stations, only 14 have been in operation before 1993 (~3%)

  • Most of the active stations (342) have been in operation for less than 20 years (~79%)

  • There are 106 stations (13%) whose operation status is unknown, which demonstrates a huge barrier to accessing information and data from solar stations!

Station tier#

The figure below shows the percentage of active stations categorized as either Tier 1 or Tier 2. It can be observed that most of the active stations are Tier 1, which means that they measure global horizontal irradiance (GHI), diffuse horizontal irradiance (DHI) using a shadow ball, and direct normal irradiance (DNI) using a pyrheliometer mounted on a solar tracker. For more information on the station tier classifications, see the station requirements page.

Finding and downloading data from solar stations can be a tedious process. The main reason is that often there is no information on how to obtain the data.
